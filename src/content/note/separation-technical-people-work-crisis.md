**00:14:01** — Separation of Technical and People Work Causes the Crisis

Annie and Michael argue that the separation between technical excellence and people-focused work in career ladders is at the root of the junior hiring crisis. When engaging with others isn't treated as a primary responsibility for all engineers, mentorship atrophies. Annie also acknowledges the capitalism dimension — the slow erosion of trust between employees and companies, where the threat of being laid off at the drop of a hat destroys loyalty and reduces the incentive for long-term investment in junior talent.
